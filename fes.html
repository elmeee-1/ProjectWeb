Discover Fès

The Spiritual Capital and Heart of Moroccan Heritage.

Landmark
The Blue Gate

Bab Boujloud

The monumental entrance to the old city. Covered in intricate Fassi blue tiles on the outside and Islamic green on the inside, it serves as the grand threshold separating the modern world from the ancient medina.

Must Visit
Living History

Chouara Tannery

One of the oldest operating leather tanneries in the world. Watch artisans process hides using centuries-old techniques in a massive courtyard filled with vibrant stone dye pits.

Culinary
Sweet & Savory

Traditional Pastilla

Fès is the birthplace of the famous Pastilla. A delicate, flaky pastry traditionally filled with pigeon or chicken, almonds, and spices, dusted with powdered sugar and cinnamon.

World Heritage
The Oldest University

Al Quaraouiyine

Founded in 859 by Fatima al-Fihri, it holds the record as the oldest continually operating university in the world. Its vast mosque and stunning courtyards represent the pinnacle of Islamic architecture.

Masterpiece
Intricate Design

Al Attarine Madrasa

Step into breathtaking geometrical perfection. This 14th-century religious school is covered from floor to ceiling in carved cedar wood, delicate stucco, and mesmerizing zellij tile work.

Shopping
The Medina Labyrinth

Souks & Crafts

The Fès el Bali medina is the largest car-free urban area in the world. Navigate through thousands of winding alleyways dedicated to distinct trades, from hammering copper and brass to weaving massive carpets.

From the bustling alleys of the world's largest medina to the colorful leather tanneries. Discover how our professional guides can help you unravel the mysteries of Fès.
