01 — Festivals

Iconic Moroccan
Celebrations

From the ancient medinas of Fez to the rose-covered valleys of the Dades, Morocco's festivals are a living tapestry of Amazigh, Arab, Andalusian, and Saharan heritage.

June · Fez
Fez Festival of World Sacred Music
Fez al-Bali, UNESCO Heritage City
Nine days of transcendence — Sufi masters, Buddhist monks, and gospel choirs in ancient medina courtyards.
May · Kelaat M'Gouna
Rose Festival
Dadès Valley, High Atlas
Three days of rose-water, parades, and Berber music celebrating the harvest of Damask roses.
June · Essaouira
Gnaoua World Music Festival
Essaouira, Atlantic Coast
Ancient Gnaoua trance rituals fuse with jazz and blues on the windswept Atlantic stage.
April · Marrakech
Popular Arts Festival
Jemaa el-Fnaa, Marrakech
Acrobats, storytellers, snake charmers, and folk musicians from every corner of Morocco.
September · Imilchil
Imilchil Marriage Festival
High Atlas, Béni Mellal-Khénifra
A centuries-old Aït Haddidou tribal moussem — one of Morocco's most intimate traditions.
November · M'Hamid el-Ghizlane
Taragalte Nomad Festival
Sahara Desert, Drâa-Tafilalet
World musicians and desert nomads share music and poetry at the edge of the great dunes.

02 — Calendar

Annual Calendar Cultural

Morocco's annual calendar includes Yennayer (Amazigh New-Year) on January 12th, celebrated with couscous, dried fruits, and family gatherings. Spring brings the Rose Festival in El-Kelaa M'Gouna, where locals dance and sell rosewater and perfumes. Other events include the almond blossom festival in Tafraout and the date festival in Erfoud, honoring harvests and local crafts.

February
🎪 Traditional Fair
Almond Blossom Festival
The valleys of Tafraoute burst into pink and white as almond trees blossom, drawing thousands to this Souss-Massa celebration of spring's arrival and Amazigh culture.
April
🎨 Arts & Heritage
Marrakech Popular Arts Festival
The UNESCO-listed Jemaa el-Fnaa square transforms into an open stage for acrobats, storytellers, musicians, and artisans from across the country.
August
🏇 Heritage & Sport
Moussem Moulay Abdellah Amghar - Tbourida
Thousands gather in El Jadida for one of Morocco's largest Fantasia gatherings: a thunderous charge of adorned horsemen, firing muskets in perfect unison in a centuries-old warrior display.
October
🎬 Film & Media
Marrakech International Film Festival
One of Africa's most glamorous film events, attracting global cinema royalty to the Red City for screenings, premieres, and retrospectives.
November
🌴 Cultural Ritual
Erfoud Date Festival
At the gateway to the Sahara, the Tafilalet oasis celebrates the date harvest with camel parades, folkloric performances, and markets overflowing with Medjool treasures.

03 — Traditions

Living Regional Traditions

Moroccan tradition is built on deep hospitality, where mint tea is a sacred daily ritual. Family, respect, and community are at its heart, celebrated through colorful festivals, traditional *djellabas*, and soulful music. Ancient crafts like zellij tilework and handwoven rugs still thrive in bustling souks..

Southern Heritage

The Gnaoua Spiritual Music

Rooted in the Sub-Saharan diaspora, the Gnaoua are masters of trance music their lila ceremonies invoke ancestral spirits through hypnotic rhythms of the guembri bass lute, qraqeb iron castanets, and collective chant. Recognised by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2019.

Amazigh Heritage

Yennayer — The Amazigh New Year

Celebrated on January 13th, Yennayer marks the Amazigh agricultural new year — a festival of family gatherings, ritual meals of couscous and dried fruit, and prayers for fertility of the land. Officially recognised as a national holiday in Morocco since 2023.

Royal Equestrian Art

Tbourida — The Fantasia Ceremony

A thundering display of horsemanship where teams of riders charge in perfect formation and fire their flintlock rifles in unison at full gallop. Rooted in medieval military exercises, Tbourida is performed at moussems across Morocco and was inscribed on UNESCO's list in 2021.

05 — Art & Music

Art &
Music

Morocco's creative soul pours through music festivals, craft fairs, and visual arts events that bridge centuries of tradition with contemporary expression.

01
Mawazine Festival
Music · Rabat · May
One of the world's largest music festivals, drawing over 2.5 million visitors to Rabat for a week of international headliners alongside Moroccan artists on 10 stages.
02
Atlas Electronic
Electronic · Marrakech · October
A groundbreaking festival fusing electronic music with traditional Moroccan sounds in the riads and rooftops of the medina — a marriage of the ancient and the future.
03
Chaabi Folk Festival
Folk Music · Casablanca · June
A vibrant celebration of Morocco's beloved Chaabi folk music, bringing together master musicians and rising voices for electrifying nights of raw, poetic street rhythms that echo through the white city.
04
Andaloussiyat Music Festival
Classical · Fès · October
A celebration of the exquisite Andalusian classical music tradition — melodic suites called nubas — performed in candlelit Fassian palaces and gardens.
05
Taragalte Nomad Festival
World Music · M'Hamid el-Ghizlane · November
At the edge of the Sahara desert, this unique festival celebrates nomadic culture and world music under an infinite canopy of desert stars.
06
Asilah Arts Festival
Visual Arts · Asilah · August
Since 1978, this Atlantic coastal town transforms its whitewashed medina walls into giant open-air canvases, with muralists, poets, and musicians from across the Arab world.
