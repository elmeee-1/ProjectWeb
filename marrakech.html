Discover Marrakech

The Red City of Magic, Spices, and Endless Energy.

Landmark
Iconic Minaret

Koutoubia Mosque

The ultimate symbol of Marrakech. This magnificent 12th-century minaret towers over the city, serving as a stunning example of Moorish architecture and a guiding compass for anyone wandering the medina.

Must Visit
The Heartbeat

Jemaa el-Fnaa

The most famous square in all of Africa. By day, it's filled with snake charmers and juice stalls. By night, it transforms into a massive open-air restaurant surrounded by storytellers, musicians, and performers.

Culinary
Local Flavor

The Authentic Tanjia

You cannot leave the Red City without trying its signature dish: Tanjia. Traditionally prepared by men and slow-cooked overnight in the ashes of the local hammam, the meat melts in your mouth with rich cumin and saffron flavors.

Architecture
Royal Elegance

Bahia Palace

A masterpiece of Moroccan design. Walk through grand courtyards, lush riad gardens, and rooms entirely covered in intricate zellij mosaics and beautifully painted cedar ceilings.

Botanical Garden
An Oasis of Blue

Jardin Majorelle

Saved from destruction by Yves Saint Laurent, this stunning botanical garden contrasts towering exotic plants with deep, striking cobalt blue architecture. It is a tranquil, artistic escape from the city streets.

Shopping
The Labyrinth

The Souks

Lose yourself in the endless, winding alleys of the old markets. From towering pyramids of spices and hanging brass lanterns to handwoven Berber carpets, the souks are a feast for every sense.
