Discover Essaouira

The Windy City of Trade, Art, and Music.

Must Visit
Historical Ramparts

Skala de la Ville

Walk along the impressive 18th-century sea walls built to protect the city. Lined with antique brass cannons, this dramatic fortress offers stunning views of the Atlantic Ocean and the medina.

Top Attraction
Coastal Life

The Fishing Port

Experience the bustling energy of the traditional fishing port. Famous for its fleet of bright blue wooden boats, seagulls circling overhead, and the authentic atmosphere of local fishermen at work.

Culinary
Authentic Dining

Fresh Seafood Stalls

You can't visit Essaouira without trying the seafood. Pick out your fresh catch directly from the stalls near the port and have it grilled to perfection right in front of you.

Landmark
UNESCO Heritage

The Medina

Get lost in the beautifully preserved Medina, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Its brilliant white walls, vibrant blue doors, and relaxed atmosphere make it completely unique compared to other Moroccan cities.

Relaxation & Sport
Wind & Sand

Essaouira Beach

A wide, sweeping beach perfect for long walks, camel rides, and watersports. Thanks to the strong coastal winds, it is renowned globally as a premier destination for kitesurfing and windsurfing.

Music & Art
Rhythms of Africa

Gnaoua Culture

Essaouira is the spiritual home of Gnaoua music, a rich tradition blending poetry, dancing, and rhythmic beats. The city comes alive with art galleries, local crafts, and impromptu street performances.
